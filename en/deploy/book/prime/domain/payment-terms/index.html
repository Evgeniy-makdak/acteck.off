Domain payment terms

When you buy a domain, you are not buying it forever, but for a certain period of time. After this, you will need to renew the domain, otherwise it will become no one's again and anyone can buy it.

As a rule, the domain is paid for1year (sometimes for several years). The rules for purchasing domains are structured in such a way that a domain cannot be purchased in large quantities. This means that you always need to make sure that the new payment is not due.

If you miss the deadline, then what happens next depends on the zone. As a rule, the domain immediately stops working, but for another month it is registered with you. And during this month you can manage to renew it. Otherwise, the domain will go on free sale.

There are, however, zones in which the domain is taken from you as soon as payment is completed. An example would be the zone.by. In such areas you need to be especially careful and not miss deadlines.

It is not entirely true that an unpaid domain goes on sale for free. If you miss the deadline and the domain is no longer yours, then you most likely will not be able to buy it back, since it will go to auction and there will be bidding for it.
