Types of sites by back part

Websites can be divided into two types: static and website with a backend.

Statics

Sites that are purely static consist only of HTML, CSS, client-side JavaScript, images and other similar resources. Such sites are easy to put on the Internet and they work very quickly.

Backend

Websites with a backend have some server-side language at their core. Typically this is PHP, or NodeJS, Python or a similar language. In addition, such sites may have a database in which site materials are stored. The database can be an SQL type, such as mySQL or Postgre, or a noSQL type, such as mongo. It is more difficult to put such sites on the Internet, and the speed of their work depends on the internal code of the site.

JavaScript frameworks

Separately, we should discuss sites that work through JavaScript frameworks: Vue, React or Angular. For such sites, NodeJS is required at the site development stage. But NodeJS is no longer needed for hosting. The fact is that before posting on the Internet, the site is assembled. The result of the assembly is ordinary static.

However, if you have routing enabled from the framework, then when uploading to the Internet without additional manipulations, routing will not work. We will study in more detail in the corresponding textbooks.
