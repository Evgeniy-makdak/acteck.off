Stages of working with virtual hosting

Suppose you already have a domain purchased from a registrar. Let this registrar be the hoster. Let's look at the principle of working with hosting.

First, you need to register for hosting. When registering, hosting usually asks you to indicate your domain. If you already have a domain and this domain was purchased from the same hoster, then the domain will automatically be parked on your hosting. If you don't have a domain, you will be prompted to register one. At this point, remember that the domaindelegated 1-2day. If you just registered it, it will not work right away.

After registration you will have access to yourpersonal accounton hosting. In it you must pay for hosting for the period you need. As a rule, the payment period can be as follows: for a month, for six months, for a year.

After payment you will have access tohosting control panels. In this panel you will manage the placement of the site on the hosting.

That is, you will have two places: your personal hosting account and the control panel.

After payment the hosting will give youFTP accessesto the site. These accesses allow you to connect to the site through a special program and upload files with code there.

FTP accesses usually come in a letter that your hosting sends you after registration. If they are not there, then you will have to create them in your hosting control panel.

Once you have FTP access, you can connect to the site and upload your files. After downloading, the site will work and you can access it via the Internet.

If you make changes to the site code on your computer, you can upload the corrected files to the hosting, and your changes will be applied to the site.

This is the main process of working with a website on hosting. We will study the nuances of this process in the following lessons. Also in the next lessons we will try everything in practice.
