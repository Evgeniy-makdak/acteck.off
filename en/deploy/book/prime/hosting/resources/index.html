Hosting Resources

As you already know, hosting is a computer located on the hoster’s premises. Our website is a program that runs on this computer, responding to user requests.

Being a program, our site eats up the resources of this computer. Here is a list of key resources:

  • hard drive space
  • RAM
  • CPU time
  • Internet channel bandwidth

Obviously, hosting is not flexible and has a limited amount of resources. Moreover, they have to be divided into many sites.

And there is a big difference when you have a regular landing page or a website with gigabytes of images or videos.

Therefore, hosting companies either significantly limit the resources allocated to one site, or charge for consumed resources according to the principlethe more you consume, the more you pay.

Exactly what you pay for depends on the type of hosting you have. We will go into more detail in the following lessons.
