Hosting for CMS

CMS, stands forcontent management system, is a special program, usually in PHP, for creating websites. Most CMS are free, but there are also paid options.

Using a CMS, you can manage a website without having any layout or programming skills, at least in theory. But to create a website, some skills will still be needed.

There are CMS for various purposes: both general purpose, for example, Wordpress, Joomla, and for a specific task. For example, Opencart and Prestashop for creating online stores. Or phpBB and MyBB for forums.

We will study working with CMS and hosting them in more detail in other textbooks. And in this lesson we’ll just discuss what opportunities there are on hosting.

In general, installing a CMS is not particularly difficult, especially if you know how to work with PHP and databases. However, hosting companies often have separate plans that provide hosting with a CMS already installed. In principle, a good thing that allows you to save some time on installation.
