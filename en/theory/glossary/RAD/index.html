RAD concept

ConceptRAD (Rapid application development, Rapid application development) facilitates rapid product development under conditions of uncertain product requirements, as well as time and budget constraints.

The implementation of the RAD concept is carried out by using technical means suitable for a specific product and constantly clarifying all requirements with the customer and involving him in assessing the intermediate stages of product readiness.

The RAD concept is based on the following principles:

  • All technical means should help minimize product development time.
  • Full focus on customer requirements for the product being developed.
  • Using product prototypes to clarify technical specifications from the customer.
  • Product development should be cyclical, i.e. each new version is created after the customer evaluates the previous version.
  • Reducing product development time by using previously prepared modules and adding functionality.
  • Close collaboration between team members, with each developer being willing to juggle multiple responsibilities at the same time.
  • The main goal of project management should be to minimize the duration of the development cycle.

Working on a project using the RAD concept includes the following stages:

  1. Planning, at which all team members discuss the technical specifications for the product being implemented, upcoming tasks and difficulties that may subsequently arise. Once planning is completed, permission must be received from the project manager to continue work.
  2. Custom designinvolves the interaction of users with analysts to subsequently clarify product requirements.
  3. Construction- the stage at which the main development of the project takes place.
  4. Switchinginvolves testing the product and its evaluation by end users.
