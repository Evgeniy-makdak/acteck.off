Internal links in HTML

In the previous lesson, we made links to pages of other people's sites by copying their address from the browser's address bar. Suppose now you want to link pages of your site with links.

In this case, you cannot copy their addresses from the address bar. The fact is that such addresses will contain the full path to your page from the root drive of your computer, and when transferred to another folder, such links will simply break.

For the pages of your site, you should take a different approach. Let's study it with an example. Let the file be located in the root folder of your siteindex.htmland filepage.html. Let's link these files with links. To do this, in the attributehreflinks, you should simply write the names of these files (with their extension). Let's do this.

On the pageindex.htmllet's link to the pagepage.html:

<h1>Index</h1> <a href="page.html">link on page</a>

And on the pagepage.htmllet's link to the pageindex.html:

<h1>Page</h1> <a href="index.html">link on index</a>

Make three HTML pages at the root of your site. Name them1.html, 2.html, 3.html. Link these pages to each other.

Also make a pageindex.html. Place links to pages on it1.html, 2.html, 3.html.

Modify the previous task so that the links are in a listul.
