Limiting greediness in regular expressions in JavaScript

Default regular expressionsgreedy. This means they capture as many characters as possible. Let's look at it with an example. Let's say we have this line:

let str = 'aeeex zzz x kkk';

Suppose we want to find a substring in this line'aeeex'according to the following template:letter'a', then any character one or more times, then a letter'x'.

let res = str.replace(/a.+x/g, '!');

We expect that the resulting string will be written to the variable'! zzz x kkk'. However, this is not so - the string ends up in the variable'! kkk'.

The thing is that our regular engine searches for all characters starting from the letter'a'to the letter'x'. But our line contains two letters'x'! Because of greed, it turns out that the regular season searches until the very last X, thereby capturing something that is not what we expected.

Of course, this is often what we need. But specifically in this case, we would like to cancel greed and tell the regular to search until the first x.

To limit greed, you need to put a question mark after the repetition operator:

let res = str.replace(/a.+?x/g, '!');

Greediness can be limited to all repetition operators: and*, And?, And{}- like this:*?, ??And{}?.

let str = 'aba accca azzza wwwwa';
