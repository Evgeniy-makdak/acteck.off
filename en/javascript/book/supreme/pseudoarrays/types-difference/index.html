Difference between pseudo-array types in JavaScript

The difference between the two types of pseudo-arrays is the different behavior when changing DOM elements. Let, for example, we receive a collection of paragraphs in the formHTMLCollectionand in the formNodeList. Next we will create and add another paragraph to our DOM. In this case, it will automatically appear inHTMLCollection, but the collectionNodeListwill remain unchanged.

Let's look at an example. Let us have a div with paragraphs in it. Let's get our div and the same paragraphs in the form of collections of two different types:

let parent = document.querySelector('div'); let elems1 = document.getElementsByTagName('p'); // HTMLCollection let elems2 = document.querySelectorAll('p'); // NodeList

Let's add another paragraph to our div:

let p = document.createElement('p'); parent.append(p);

As a result, one of the collections will change, but the other will not:

console.log(elems1); // 4 абзаца - с новым console.log(elems2); // 3 начальных абзаца
